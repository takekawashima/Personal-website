Projects

Praxis II Engineering Design Project (2023)

×

As part of the Praxis I Engineering Design Project, our team partnered with the Swim Drink Fish (SDF) Toronto Hub, a non-profit organization that monitors water quality across Canada. After attending a winter field session with SDF, we identified key pain points in their data collection process‚Äîspecifically, the vulnerability of paper-based forms to wet weather and the time-consuming nature of manually entering observations into a digital database.

To address these challenges, we developed SlideXCell, an integrated physical-digital prototype of the solution. The system features a waterproof slider interface for recording environmental observations on-site, paired with a custom-built mobile app that digitally scans and logs slider input. Unlike existing solutions that address each problem independently, our design tackles both challenges simultaneously to streamline fieldwork.

The design was finalized through regular feedback from SDF and iterative prototyping, we ensured compatibility with their existing data collection formats and workflows.

Our team was awarded first place in the design competition, ranking highest among all first-year Engineering Science teams.

Walker's Group ‚ÄîCanadian Arctic ACE/OSIRIS Validation Campaign (Summer 2024)

During the summer of 2024, I participated in the Canadian Arctic ACE/OSIRIS Validation Campaigns to investigate factors influencing the variability in ozonesonde burst altitudes using nearly two decades of atmospheric data. Leveraging Python and Pandas, I analyzed correlations between burst altitude and environmental conditions such as tropopause temperature, wind, and vertical ascent rate. This project strengthened my skills in large-scale data processing and highlighted the complex interplay of environmental and mechanical factors in high-altitude balloon measurements under extreme Arctic conditions.

During my summer research at the Institute for Advanced Biosciences, Keio University, I contributed to the genome sequencing and analysis of Actinoplanes sichuanensis, a bacterium with potential for producing acarbose‚Äîan anti-diabetic compound. My work involved wet lab techniques such as bacterial culturing, high-molecular-weight DNA extraction, and quality verification using TapeStation.
